Company Background

GMM Ltd is a UK-registered company that has operated in data-driven services since its incorporation. The company's primary business has been in education marketing and university recruitment, operating the platform StudyQA.com, a service connecting international students with universities worldwide. StudyQA serves over 4 million annual users across 100+ countries and partners with hundreds of universities globally. Through this platform, GMM has built deep expertise in large-scale data collection, processing, analytics, and product development serving an international user base.

In 2025, GMM began a strategic pivot towards AI and financial data applications. The company identified an opportunity to apply its data engineering capabilities to UK Companies House filings, specifically the challenge of extracting structured financial data from millions of PDF company accounts that are not available in machine-readable form.

This led to GMM applying for and being awarded a UKRI AIRR compute grant (20,000 GPU hours on the Isambard-AI supercomputer at the University of Bristol) to research document AI approaches for financial document understanding. The AIRR award (reference 0261-4941-0401-1) is active from April to July 2026. UKRI approval of the AIRR compute allocation provides external validation of the project's research relevance and technical feasibility.

Technical Capabilities and Progress

Since receiving the AIRR compute grant, GMM has made significant progress on the Companies House data project:

  • iXBRL parsing: 815,000 structured filings from the Companies House bulk download have been parsed into a normalised data format, serving as ground truth for model training and validation.
  • PDF acquisition: Approximately 3.5 million PDF filings have been downloaded from Companies House via an automated pipeline, representing an estimated 48% incremental coverage beyond the iXBRL dataset.
  • Financial scoring model: A LightGBM classifier trained on the extracted iXBRL features achieves AUC 0.9987 for company financial health prediction, validating the quality of the structured data.
  • Document AI pilot: Feasibility of field extraction from PDF filings has been confirmed using a Donut-class (vision-transformer) baseline model.
