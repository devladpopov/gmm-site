Project Overview

GMM Ltd and University College London propose a £1.3M project under the DSIT Sovereign AI Strategic Assets Programme (non-commercial track, 100% funded) to create a national open AI dataset from UK Companies House financial filings.

Companies House holds records on approximately 5.4 million registered companies. While around 815,000 filings are available as structured iXBRL data, millions more exist only as PDF documents. This project bridges the gap by training document AI models to extract structured financial data from the PDF long tail, creating a single unified dataset covering the full UK company population.

GMM Ltd has already secured a UKRI AIRR compute grant (20,000 GPU hours on Isambard-AI, University of Bristol), parsed 815K iXBRL filings into structured ground truth, and downloaded approximately 3.5 million PDF filings from Companies House. The project is ready for the research and production phase.

The Problem

Companies House is the UK’s central register of company information, holding records on approximately 5.4 million companies. While a proportion of financial accounts are available as structured iXBRL data (approximately 815,000 in the bulk download), a substantial long tail of historical, micro-entity, small-company and PDF-only filings remains locked in unstructured documents.

These PDF filings often contain valuable financial indicators—turnover, assets, liabilities, profit/loss—but are not available as clean, normalised, AI-ready data. This creates a gap for researchers, regulators, fintech firms and AI startups: the companies most relevant to SME risk, productivity and regional economic analysis are often the hardest to analyse systematically.

No open dataset currently unifies both iXBRL and PDF-only filings into a single, consistent, machine-readable resource covering the full UK company population. Commercial alternatives cost tens of thousands per year, do not cover micro-companies comprehensively, and are not available for open research. The UK Government already invests significantly in proprietary entity-resolution and decision intelligence platforms for Companies House data (e.g. the SNAP platform operated by the Public Sector Fraud Authority). Gartner now recognises Decision Intelligence as a distinct market category, underscoring the strategic value of structured company data. An open dataset would democratise access to this capability for the broader research and innovation ecosystem.

Our Solution

We propose to construct a national open-access structured financial dataset covering all UK registered companies. Where filings are already available in iXBRL, existing structured data are parsed and normalised. For the substantial PDF-only long tail, a document intelligence model is trained to read page images and extract financial fields into the same consistent machine-readable format, closing the coverage gap.

2 Document Classification
3 Page Selection & OCR
4 Table & Field Localisation
5 Schema Normalisation
6 Accounting Validation
7 JSON + Confidence
8 Open Dataset & API

The production system uses a validation-first document intelligence pipeline combining page classification, table/field localisation, OCR/text-layer extraction, schema normalisation, and deterministic accounting checks. Every extracted value carries source evidence, page reference, bounding box where available, confidence score, and validation status.

Machine learning components, trained on verified iXBRL ground truth, operate within this controlled pipeline rather than as standalone generative extractors. Early pilot work with a Donut-class baseline model confirmed field identification feasibility. The production architecture adds the validation and governance layers required for a national-scale trusted dataset.

Project Deliverables

  • Open dataset: structured financial data for UK companies in JSON format, freely downloadable under open licence (CC BY or equivalent)
  • Trained AI model: open-weight document understanding model optimised for UK financial reports
  • Public API: RESTful interface for querying company financial data by registration number
  • Research publications: at least two peer-reviewed papers submitted to NLP, document AI, or financial data science venues
  • Benchmark suite: evaluation framework for financial document extraction accuracy
  • Postdoctoral research outputs: methodology papers and evaluation reports from UCL-embedded research programme
  • Technical documentation: full methodology, data dictionary, and reproduction guide

Governance and Quality Assurance

  • Open Data Management Plan: FAIR-compliant data management with clear licensing, access, and reuse terms
  • Reproducible extraction pipeline: fully documented, containerised pipeline enabling independent reproduction of results
  • Versioned dataset releases: semantic versioning with changelogs; each release immutable and citable
  • Field-level accuracy benchmarks: per-field precision, recall, and confidence scores published with each release
  • Bias and coverage analysis: systematic analysis by company type, sector, geography, and filing year
  • Audit trail: each extracted value linked to source document page and extraction confidence
  • Public documentation and data dictionary: comprehensive schema documentation, field definitions, and usage guides
  • Legal opinion (pre-submission): formal legal opinion on Companies House data reuse, Crown Copyright compliance, and bulk-access arrangements

Why It Matters

No equivalent open, AI-ready dataset exists for extracting and validating the PDF-only long tail of UK company accounts at national scale. Commercial alternatives cost tens of thousands per year and do not cover micro-companies comprehensively.

This dataset makes financial transparency and corporate accountability a public good, accessible to every UK researcher, regulator, and innovator.

Financial Crime & Corporate Accountability

Structured financial data across all UK companies enables evidence-based detection of fraudulent reporting, shell company networks, and money laundering patterns. The dataset supports a shift from reactive case-by-case analysis to proactive, data-driven monitoring of corporate financial behaviour.

SME Credit & Economic Research

Cross-referencing financial statements across the full company population enables new research into SME credit risk, regional economic productivity, and corporate health patterns. UCL’s expertise in financial data science and risk analytics directly supports this research dimension.

Commercial Trajectory & IP

The DSIT programme explicitly encourages grantees to retain and commercially exploit IP created during the project. This proposal maximises public benefit while enabling sustainable commercial development.

Open Foundation Layer

The core dataset and base extraction model are released as open access under a permissive licence (CC BY or equivalent), creating sovereign UK AI infrastructure that any researcher, regulator, or startup can use freely.

GMM Commercial Layer

GMM retains foreground IP in the extraction pipeline and commercialises value-added services:

  • Premium API: high-throughput, SLA-backed access for fintech
  • Real-time updates: continuous ingestion of new filings
  • Custom models: domain-specific fine-tuning (credit, fraud, ESG)
  • Data enrichment: cross-referencing with HMRC, Land Registry, FCA

Government retains usage rights. The open dataset serves public good; GMM’s commercial layer creates a self-sustaining UK AI company anchored to a sovereign data asset.
